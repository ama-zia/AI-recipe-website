Hazelnut Chocolate Cupcakes

These hazelnut chocolate cupcakes are the kind of treat that makes you pause mid-bite and smile. The deep cocoa richness pairs perfectly with the nutty crunch of toasted hazelnuts, and the soft crumb stays moist thanks to a clever mix of oil, hot coffee, and milk. It’s a deceptively simple recipe — but nailing the texture depends on not overmixing your batter, and keeping a close eye near the end of the bake so you don’t dry them out. The frosting brings everything together with a silky chocolate hazelnut blend that’s rich but not overpowering.

Whether you're baking for a special occasion or just a quiet evening in, these cupcakes deliver a luxurious, homemade feel without any hassle. And if you’re working with dietary restrictions, no worries — our built-in AI chatbot can help you instantly adapt this recipe. It’ll suggest gluten-free flour swaps, vegan alternatives for the egg and dairy, or even lower-sugar options that still taste amazing. Just ask.

Serving size: Makes 10–12 cupcakes

Bake time: 18–22 minutes at 175°C / 350°F

Cooling time: Allow to cool completely before frosting

Cupcake Ingredients

  • ¾ cup (95g) all-purpose flour
  • ¼ cup (25g) unsweetened cocoa powder (Dutch process)
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ¼ tsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ½ cup (120ml) whole milk (or plain yogurt)
  • ½ tsp vanilla extract
  • ½ cup (100g) granulated sugar
  • ¼ cup (50g) brown sugar
  • ⅓ cup (80ml) vegetable oil
  • 1 large egg
  • ½ cup (120ml) hot brewed coffee or hot water
  • ⅓ cup (40g) finely chopped toasted hazelnuts
  • Optional: ¼ cup chopped dark chocolate chunks or chips

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 175°C / 350°F. Line cupcake tray with 10–12 liners.
  2. Sift together flour, cocoa, baking soda, baking powder, salt. Stir in chopped hazelnuts.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together milk, vanilla, egg, both sugars, and oil.
  4. Stir in hot coffee (or hot water). Batter will be thin — that's perfect.
  5. Pour wet into dry and mix just until smooth. Fold in chocolate chunks if using.
  6. Fill liners ¾ full. Bake for 18–22 minutes until a skewer comes out with moist crumbs.
  7. Cool completely before frosting.

Hazelnut Chocolate Frosting

  • ½ cup (115g) unsalted butter, softened
  • ⅓ cup (90g) chocolate hazelnut spread (like Nutella)
  • 1¼ cups (150g) powdered sugar
  • 2 tbsp cocoa powder (optional)
  • 2 tbsp heavy cream or milk
  • Pinch of salt
  • ½ tsp vanilla extract

Method:

  1. Beat butter until light and fluffy (2–3 minutes).
  2. Add Nutella and beat again until smooth.
  3. Sift in powdered sugar and cocoa. Beat until creamy.
  4. Add cream, vanilla, and salt. Whip until fluffy and pipeable.
  5. Optional: Add melted dark chocolate or espresso for deeper flavor.
