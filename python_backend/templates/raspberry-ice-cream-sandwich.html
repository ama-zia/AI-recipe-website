Raspberry Ice Cream Sandwich

There’s something quietly brilliant about a homemade raspberry ice cream sandwich. It’s not just the contrast between creamy and chewy or the sweet and tart — it’s the way each bite feels like summer wrapped between two cookies. You don’t need professional gear or fancy ingredients. What you need is a little patience, quality vanilla, ripe raspberries, and a cookie base that knows how to hold everything together without falling apart. The real trick? Letting the cookies cool completely before sandwiching, and freezing the assembled treats just long enough to set — but not so long they become rock hard. This recipe walks that line perfectly.

Serving Size: Makes 5–6 sandwiches (10–12 cookies)

Baking Time: 8–10 minutes

Oven Temperature: 175°C / 350°F

Cooling Time: At least 30 minutes for cookies, 6+ hours freeze time for ice cream

Ingredients

For the Oat Cookie Base (makes 10–12 cookies):

  • 1 cup (90g) rolled oats
  • ¾ cup (95g) all-purpose flour
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ cup (115g) soft butter or coconut oil
  • ½ cup (100g) brown sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Optional: ½ tsp cinnamon

For the Raspberry Compote:

  • 1 cup raspberries (fresh or frozen)
  • 1–2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • Optional: pinch of salt or vanilla

For the No-Churn Vanilla Ice Cream:

  • 1½ cups (360ml) cold heavy cream
  • ½ can (200g) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1½ tsp vanilla extract (or ½ tsp vanilla bean paste)
  • Optional: pinch of salt

Step-by-Step Method

1. Make the Oat Cookies:

  1. Preheat oven to 175°C / 350°F. Line tray with baking paper.
  2. Cream butter and sugar until fluffy. Add egg and vanilla.
  3. Add oats, flour, baking soda, salt. Stir until a soft dough forms.
  4. Scoop ~1½ tbsp dough balls, flatten slightly.
  5. Bake 8–10 mins until golden edges and soft centers.
  6. Cool completely — they’ll firm up.

2. Make Raspberry Compote:

  1. Simmer raspberries, sugar, lemon juice for 6–8 mins in a small pot.
  2. Cool fully. It will thicken as it chills.
  3. Refrigerate until needed.

3. Make No-Churn Vanilla Ice Cream:

  1. Whip cold cream to soft peaks.
  2. In another bowl, mix condensed milk and vanilla.
  3. Fold whipped cream into milk mix gently (don’t deflate!).
  4. Pour into a loaf pan and swirl in raspberry compote (optional).
  5. Freeze for 6+ hours or overnight.

4. Assemble the Sandwiches:

  1. Scoop thick ice cream onto one oat cookie.
  2. Top with a spoonful of raspberry compote.
  3. Place another cookie on top and gently press.
  4. Freeze sandwiches 15–30 minutes before eating.

Berry dreamy, creamy, and chewy!
