Carrot Cake with Vanilla Cream Cheese Frosting

This carrot cake has earned its place in the baking hall of fame—not just because it's incredibly moist and packed with flavor, but because it's surprisingly easy to get just right. It strikes that rare balance between spice and sweetness, between hearty and delicate, and it holds up whether you're baking for a crowd or treating yourself on a quiet afternoon.

What really makes this cake shine is the pairing of finely grated carrots, crushed pineapple, and toasted pecans, giving it a complexity of texture that plays beautifully with the rich cream cheese frosting. If you're nervous about baking, don't be—this one is forgiving. Focus on not overmixing the batter and allowing it to cool fully before layering and frosting. The results will speak for themselves.

Serving Size: 10–12 slices

Bake Time: 30–35 minutes

Temperature: 175°C / 350°F

Cooling Time: Let cool completely before frosting (about 1 hour)

Ingredients

For the Cake:

  • 2 cups (260g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 ½ tsp baking soda
  • 1 ½ tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 ½ tsp cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp nutmeg (optional)
  • 1 cup (200g) brown sugar, packed
  • ½ cup (100g) white sugar
  • 1 cup (240ml) neutral oil (like canola or sunflower)
  • 4 large eggs, room temp
  • 1 tbsp pure vanilla extract
  • 2 cups (200g) finely grated carrots
  • ¾ cup (90g) toasted pecans, chopped
  • ½ cup (120g) crushed pineapple, drained
  • Optional: zest of ½ orange

For the Intense Vanilla Cream Cheese Frosting:

  • 225g (8 oz) cream cheese, cold
  • ½ cup (115g) unsalted butter, room temp
  • 2 cups (240g) powdered sugar, sifted
  • 1 tbsp vanilla bean paste or 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Step-by-Step Method

Step 1: Bake the Cake

  1. Preheat oven to 175°C / 350°F. Grease and line two 8-inch round pans or one 9x13 inch.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk flour, baking soda, baking powder, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg.
  3. In another bowl, whisk eggs, brown sugar, white sugar, oil, and vanilla until smooth and glossy.
  4. Fold in grated carrots, pineapple, and toasted pecans.
  5. Gently mix wet and dry ingredients until just combined — don’t overmix.
  6. Divide batter evenly between pans and bake for 30–35 minutes, or until a toothpick comes out clean.
  7. Let the cakes cool fully before frosting.

Step 2: Make the Vanilla Cream Cheese Frosting

  1. Beat cream cheese until smooth and creamy.
  2. Add butter and beat until light and fluffy.
  3. Gradually add powdered sugar, beating after each cup.
  4. Add vanilla and a pinch of salt. Whip until fluffy.
  5. Chill for 10–15 minutes if needed before spreading.

Step 3: Assemble

  1. Place one cake layer on a plate or stand. Spread a thick layer of frosting.
  2. Top with the second layer. Frost top and sides generously.
  3. Decorate with chopped pecans, carrot curls, or a sprinkle of cinnamon.
