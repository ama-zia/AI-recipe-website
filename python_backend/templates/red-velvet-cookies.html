Red Velvet Cookies

These red velvet cookies aren’t just another version of a popular cake — they’re a full-on indulgence: soft in the center, lightly crisp on the edges, and rich with that signature cocoa-vanilla depth. The bold color and classic cream cheese frosting make them feel bakery-worthy, yet they're easy enough for a weekday bake. If you're looking to impress or simply treat yourself, mastering the chill time and not overbaking are key. Letting them cool on the tray helps retain that soft, dense texture. Serving size: 10–12 cookies

Baking temperature: 175°C / 350°F

Baking time: 9–11 minutes

Cooling time: 5 minutes on tray, then transfer to rack

Ingredients

For the Red Velvet Cookies (makes ~10–12):

  • ½ cup (115g) soft unsalted butter
  • ¾ cup (150g) brown sugar
  • ¼ cup (50g) white sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tbsp red food coloring (gel preferred)
  • 1 tbsp unsweetened cocoa powder (not Dutch-processed)
  • 1½ cups (190g) all-purpose flour
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • Optional: ½ cup white chocolate chips or chunks

For the Cream Cheese Frosting:

  • 115g (½ cup) cream cheese, softened
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter, softened
  • ¾ cup (75g) powdered sugar, sifted
  • ½ tsp vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt
  • Optional: 1 tsp lemon zest

Method

STEP 1: Make the Cookie Dough

  1. Cream butter and sugars until fluffy.
  2. Add egg, vanilla, red food coloring. Mix well.
  3. Sift in flour, cocoa, baking soda, and salt. Mix until dough forms.
  4. Optional: Fold in white chocolate chips.
  5. Chill dough 30–60 mins for best texture.

STEP 2: Bake the Cookies

  1. Preheat oven to 175°C / 350°F. Line baking tray.
  2. Scoop 1.5 tbsp dough balls. Space them apart.
  3. Bake for 9–11 mins until edges are set, centers soft.
  4. Cool 5 mins on tray, then move to rack.

STEP 3: Make the Frosting

  1. Beat cream cheese and butter until smooth.
  2. Add powdered sugar, vanilla, and salt. Beat until creamy.
  3. Chill frosting 10–15 mins for thicker texture.

STEP 4: Decorate!

  1. Once cookies are cool, pipe or dollop frosting on top.
  2. Optional: sprinkle with cookie crumbs or white choc shavings.

Soft, rich, frosted — red velvet perfection!
