Blueberry Lemon Sourdough Boule

The Blueberry Lemon Sourdough Boule is a unique twist on traditional sourdough, combining the tang of lemon zest with the juicy sweetness of blueberries. It's not just a loaf; it's a celebration of contrasts—crisp crust meets tender crumb, fruity brightness meets earthy fermentation. It might seem like a bakery-level challenge, but with careful attention to temperature and fermentation timing, even a home baker can achieve that signature open crumb and vibrant flavor.

Make sure your sourdough starter is active and bubbly before you start. Don’t rush the cold proof—this step develops the texture and flavor that make this boule so memorable. If your dough feels tricky to handle after adding berries, use wet hands and a gentle touch to keep from tearing the gluten structure. Serving Size: Makes 1 medium boule (6–8 slices)

Baking Time: 20 minutes covered, then 20–25 minutes uncovered

Oven Temperature: 260°C / 500°F (preheat) → 230°C / 450°F (for uncovered bake)

Cool Time: 1–2 hours before slicing

Ingredients

For the Dough:

  • 400g bread flour
  • 100g whole wheat flour
  • 375g water (warm, ~80–85°F / 27–29°C)
  • 100g active sourdough starter
  • 9g (1½ tsp) fine sea salt

Add-ins (after bulk starts):

  • 150g fresh or frozen blueberries (don’t thaw)
  • Zest of 1–2 lemons
  • Optional: 1 tbsp lemon juice

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Mix Dough (Autolyse): In a large bowl, mix flours and water until just combined. Cover and rest 45–60 minutes.
  2. Add Starter & Salt: Add sourdough starter and salt. Use stretch & folds or kneading to mix for 3–5 minutes.
  3. Bulk Fermentation (4–5 hrs): Do 4 stretch & folds every 30 minutes. Add blueberries and lemon zest on 2nd or 3rd fold. Handle gently.
  4. Pre-Shape & Bench Rest: Turn dough onto a floured surface. Shape gently into a round and let rest uncovered for 20 min.
  5. Final Shape: Shape into tight boule. Place in floured banneton or towel-lined bowl dusted with rice flour.
  6. Cold Proof: Cover and refrigerate for 12–18 hours.
  7. Bake:
    • Preheat oven to 500°F / 260°C with Dutch oven inside.
    • Place dough on parchment, score top.
    • Bake 20 min covered, then 20–25 min uncovered at 450°F / 230°C.
  8. Cool: Cool completely (1–2 hours) before slicing for best texture.

Bright, tangy, and rustic — this sourdough is perfect for breakfast or a cozy brunch
