Date Cake

A good date cake is more than just a sweet treat—it's the kind of dessert that feels like a quiet win. With its deep, caramel-like flavor from the softened dates, a soft crumb from the yogurt and whole wheat flour, and a satisfying crunch from the walnuts, this cake finds the right balance between indulgent and nourishing. Whether you’re baking for guests or keeping it all to yourself for the week ahead, nailing this cake means paying attention to a few simple details: don’t skip soaking the dates, mix the wet and dry ingredients with care, and don’t overbake it. The result? A moist, golden loaf that slices beautifully and doesn’t even need frosting—though a light yogurt glaze takes it to another level if you’re feeling extra. Serving size: One loaf or 8-inch round cake

Baking temperature: 175°C / 350°F

Baking time: 35–45 minutes

Cooling time: 10 minutes in pan, then fully on wire rack

Ingredients (for 1 loaf or 8-inch cake)

Dry:

  • 1 cup (120g) whole wheat flour
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp cinnamon (optional)

Date-Walnut Mix:

  • 1½ cups (225g) chopped soft dates
  • ½ cup (120ml) hot water
  • ¼ tsp baking soda
  • ½ cup (60g) chopped walnuts

Wet:

  • ¼ cup (60ml) oil
  • ¼ cup (60g) plain yogurt
  • 2 tbsp (30ml) milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • ¼ cup (50g) brown sugar (or coconut sugar)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Soften Dates: In a bowl, mix dates, hot water, and ¼ tsp baking soda. Let sit 10 mins, then mash lightly. Set aside.
  2. Mix Wet Ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk eggs, yogurt, oil, milk, vanilla, and sugar. Stir in softened dates.
  3. Mix Dry Ingredients: In another bowl, whisk flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and cinnamon.
  4. Combine: Fold dry ingredients into wet just until combined. Gently stir in walnuts.
  5. Bake: Preheat oven to 175°C / 350°F. Grease and line a loaf pan or 8-inch round tin. Pour in batter. Bake 35–45 mins until a skewer comes out clean or with moist crumbs.
  6. Cool: Let cake rest in the pan 10 minutes, then cool fully on a wire rack.

Optional Yogurt Glaze

  • 2 tbsp Greek yogurt
  • 1–2 tbsp honey or maple syrup
  • 1–2 tbsp milk (to thin)

Method: Whisk all glaze ingredients until smooth. Pour over cooled cake and let set.
