Gingerbread Cookies

These gingerbread cookies are the kind of nostalgic treat that brings warmth to any cold day. They're everything a good cookie should be: lightly crisp on the edges, soft in the middle, and full of the deep, spicy notes of molasses, cinnamon, ginger, and cloves. Whether you're baking them for a festive gathering, decorating them with kids, or simply making a batch to snack on throughout the week, this recipe delivers consistent results. The key to acing these cookies is in the chilling and rolling — don’t rush those steps. Chilled dough means clean cookie cutter shapes and even baking, while rolling to just the right thickness keeps texture perfect.

  • Yields: ~24 cookies
  • Prep Time: 20 minutes
  • Chill Time: 1–2 hours
  • Bake Time: 8–10 minutes
  • Oven Temperature: 175°C / 350°F
  • Cool Time: 10–15 minutes

Ingredients

  • 3 cups (375g) all-purpose flour
  • ¾ cup (150g) dark brown sugar
  • ¾ cup (180ml) unsulphured molasses
  • 1 large egg
  • ½ cup (113g) unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp ground ginger
  • 1 tbsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ tsp ground cloves
  • ½ tsp nutmeg
  • Optional: ½ tsp allspice
  • Optional: 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Cream Butter and Sugar: Beat butter and brown sugar until light and fluffy (2–3 minutes).
  2. Add Wet Ingredients: Mix in egg, molasses, and vanilla if using. Beat until smooth.
  3. Add Dry Ingredients: In a separate bowl, whisk flour, baking soda, salt, and spices. Gradually mix into the wet ingredients until a dough forms.
  4. Chill: Divide dough into two disks, wrap in cling film, and chill for 1–2 hours (or overnight).

Rolling and Baking

  1. Roll and Cut: Preheat oven to 175°C / 350°F. Roll dough on floured surface to ¼ inch thick. Cut into festive shapes.
  2. Bake: Place on lined tray and bake for 8–10 minutes. Cool completely before icing or decorating.

Tips for Success

  • Chill the dough well — this helps clean cuts and keeps cookies from spreading.
  • Use dark brown sugar and unsulphured molasses for rich flavor.
  • Roll evenly for consistent baking — ¼ inch is the sweet spot.
  • For soft cookies, bake 7–8 minutes; for crisp ones, bake closer to 10 minutes.

These cookies are perfect for decorating with royal icing or enjoying just as they are.
