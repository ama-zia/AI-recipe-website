Salted Caramel Banana Cake

This Salted Caramel Banana Cake is the kind of dessert that gets better with every bite. The moistness from ripe bananas, combined with the richness of Greek yogurt and brown sugar, gives this cake a naturally sweet, deeply satisfying texture. It's the perfect balance between comforting banana bread and indulgent cake. And the salted caramel frosting? It’s not just a topping—it’s a creamy, sweet-salty finish that takes the whole thing to a new level. If you’ve got overripe bananas on the counter, don’t overthink it—this is the recipe they’re meant for.

Serving Size: 8–10 slices

Baking Temperature: 175°C / 350°F

Baking Time: 28–35 minutes

Cooling Time: Allow to cool completely before frosting (about 1 hour)

Ingredients

For the Banana Cake:

  • 1 ½ cups (180g) whole wheat flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 tsp cinnamon (optional)
  • ½ cup (115g) unsalted butter, softened
  • ½ cup (100g) brown sugar
  • ¼ cup (60ml) honey or maple syrup
  • 2 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • ½ cup (120g) Greek yogurt or sour cream
  • 3 medium ripe bananas, mashed (about 1 ¼ cups)

For the Simple Salted Caramel Frosting:

  • ½ cup (100g) brown sugar
  • ¼ cup (60g) unsalted butter
  • 2 tbsp milk or cream
  • Pinch of sea salt
  • 1 cup (120g) powdered sugar, sifted
  • Optional: extra sea salt for topping

Step-by-Step Directions

Step 1: Bake the Cake

  1. Preheat oven to 175°C / 350°F. Grease and line an 8-inch pan.
  2. Whisk whole wheat flour, baking soda, baking powder, salt, and cinnamon together.
  3. Cream butter, brown sugar, and honey until light and fluffy (2–3 minutes).
  4. Add eggs one at a time, beating well after each. Stir in vanilla.
  5. Mix in mashed bananas and Greek yogurt.
  6. Fold in dry ingredients until just combined—do not overmix.
  7. Pour into the prepared pan and bake for 28–35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted comes out clean.
  8. Cool completely before frosting.

Step 2: Make the Salted Caramel Frosting

  1. In a small saucepan, melt butter and brown sugar over medium heat.
  2. Stir constantly until bubbly (2–3 minutes). Add milk or cream and salt.
  3. Let it bubble for 1 minute, then remove from heat and let cool for 5–10 minutes.
  4. Stir in powdered sugar until smooth.
  5. Adjust consistency with more sugar or a few drops of milk if needed.
  6. Spread over the cooled cake. Sprinkle with extra salt if desired.
