Brioche Bread

Brioche is one of those magical baked goods that sits at the intersection of bread and dessert. It's rich with butter and eggs, lightly sweet, and impossibly soft inside with a golden crust that feels like something you’d only get at a French bakery. If you’ve ever hesitated to bake bread from scratch, let this be your gateway. It’s a forgiving dough if you take your time, and the result is worth every minute. The key to acing this recipe lies in the kneading — don’t rush it. Let the dough pull together fully before adding the butter, and don’t skip the cold overnight fermentation step; it deepens the flavor and improves the texture. Use a kitchen scale for best accuracy and don’t be afraid of sticky dough — it’s meant to be that way.

Serving size: 1 loaf or 12 buns

Baking temperature: 180°C / 350°F

Baking time: Loaf: 25–30 minutes | Buns: 18–20 minutes

Cooling time: Let cool for at least 20 minutes before slicing

Ingredients

Dry:

  • 3 ¼ cups (400g) bread flour
  • 2 tbsp (25g) granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp salt

Wet:

  • 3 large eggs (room temp)
  • ⅓ cup (80ml) warm milk
  • 2 ¼ tsp (7g) instant yeast
  • 170g (¾ cup) unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract (optional)

Egg Wash:

  • 1 egg + 1 tbsp milk, beaten

Instructions

  1. Activate Yeast: Mix warm milk, yeast, and 1 tsp sugar. Let sit 5–10 min until foamy (optional step for instant yeast).
  2. Make Dough: In stand mixer, combine flour, sugar, and salt. Add eggs, yeast mixture, and vanilla. Mix on low for 2–3 min until rough dough forms.
  3. Knead + Add Butter: Knead on medium 5–6 min. Add softened butter a spoonful at a time. Knead 10–12 more min until dough is smooth, stretchy, shiny, and slightly sticky.
  4. First Rise: Place dough in greased bowl, cover, and rise for 1.5–2 hrs until doubled.
  5. Cold Fermentation: Punch down, wrap, and refrigerate overnight (or at least 6 hrs).
  6. Shape: For loaf: divide into 3–4 balls and place in buttered loaf pan. For buns: divide into 12 pieces and roll into balls.
  7. Second Rise: Let shaped dough rise 1.5–2 hrs until puffy and doubled.
  8. Bake: Preheat oven to 180°C / 350°F. Brush with egg wash.
    • Loaf: bake 25–30 min
    • Buns: bake 18–20 min
    • Cover loosely with foil if browning too fast
  9. Cool & Serve: Let cool 20 min before slicing. Enjoy with butter, jam, or lightly toasted.
