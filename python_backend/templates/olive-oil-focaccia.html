Olive Oil Focaccia

Olive oil focaccia is one of those humble yet magical bakes that delivers far more than the sum of its parts. With its crisp golden crust, airy crumb, and rich olive oil flavor, it’s a bread that feels both rustic and refined. Whether served warm with dips, layered into a sandwich, or enjoyed as is, it never fails to impress. The key to nailing focaccia lies in letting the dough ferment slowly overnight — that long rest builds the signature bubbles and softness. Don’t skimp on the olive oil either; it’s not just for taste but also helps develop the beautiful crust and prevents sticking. Serving Size: Makes one 9x13" tray (serves 6–8)

Baking Temperature: 220°C / 425°F

Bake Time: 22–28 minutes

Cooling Time: 10–15 minutes

Ingredients

Dough:

  • 500g (4 cups) bread flour (or all-purpose)
  • 10g (2 tsp) fine sea salt
  • 2 tsp sugar or honey
  • 2 tsp instant yeast (or 7g active dry)
  • 400ml (1⅔ cups) warm water (~105°F / 40°C)
  • 3 tbsp extra virgin olive oil (plus more for pan and topping)

Toppings (choose or mix):

  • Flaky salt
  • Fresh rosemary
  • Cherry tomatoes (halved)
  • Sliced garlic
  • Caramelized onions
  • Olives
  • Chili flakes
  • Fresh thyme, oregano, or basil (after baking)

Dough Instructions

  1. Mix the Dough: In a large bowl, stir together flour, salt, sugar, and yeast. Add warm water and olive oil. Mix into a sticky dough (no kneading needed).
  2. First Rise (Bulk): Cover and let rise at room temp for 8–12 hours (overnight is great). Dough should triple and look bubbly.
  3. Pan & Proof: Generously oil a 9x13" pan (2–3 tbsp). Pour in the jiggly dough and gently stretch it to fit. Let rise 2–4 hours until very puffy.

Topping & Baking

  1. Dimple & Top: Preheat oven to 220°C / 425°F. Drizzle 2–3 tbsp olive oil over dough. Use oiled fingers to dimple deeply. Add flaky salt, rosemary, and any toppings.
  2. Bake: Bake 22–28 mins until golden on top and crisp underneath. Cool 10–15 minutes before slicing.

Perfect for dipping, sandwiches, or just eating warm with olive oil. Simple and satisfying.
