Blueberry Macarons

Blueberry macarons are one of those desserts that seem intimidating until you actually give them a fair shot. They demand precision, patience, and care — but once you nail that first perfect batch with its crisp shell and soft chewy center, you'll understand why so many bakers chase the macaron high. The balance of textures and the burst of fresh blueberry flavor in the filling make these little bites unforgettable.

To get consistent results, always weigh your ingredients and don’t skip the resting period for the shells. These tiny steps make a huge difference. It’s like baking with a smart, supportive friend who gets it.

  • Yields: 20–24 macarons
  • Prep time: 1 hour
  • Rest time: 30–60 minutes
  • Bake time: 12–15 minutes
  • Temperature: 150°C / 300°F (no fan)
  • Cool time: Let cool fully before removing from tray

Shell Ingredients

  • 100g almond flour (super fine)
  • 100g powdered sugar
  • 90g egg whites (about 3, aged 24 hrs)
  • 90g granulated sugar
  • Gel food coloring: violet or blue + red
  • Optional: ¼ tsp cream of tartar

Blueberry Buttercream Filling

  • ½ cup (75g) fresh or frozen blueberries
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • 115g unsalted butter (room temp)
  • 1½ cups (180g) powdered sugar
  • Pinch of salt
  • Optional: 1 tsp blueberry extract

Step 1: Make the Macaron Shells

  1. Sift almond flour and powdered sugar together. Discard any coarse bits.
  2. Whip egg whites until foamy. Add cream of tartar (if using).
  3. Gradually add granulated sugar and whip until stiff peaks form (5–6 mins).
  4. Add gel food coloring to create a vibrant blueberry shade.
  5. Fold dry ingredients into meringue gently. Batter should flow like lava and form ribbons.
  6. Pipe 1.5-inch rounds onto lined tray. Tap tray to release air bubbles.
  7. Rest 30–60 minutes until a skin forms and they’re no longer sticky.

Step 2: Bake

  1. Preheat oven to 150°C / 300°F (no fan).
  2. Bake 12–15 minutes, rotating halfway. Let cool fully before removing.

Step 3: Make Blueberry Buttercream

  1. Simmer blueberries + lemon juice until thick (5–7 mins). Push through sieve and cool.
  2. Beat butter until fluffy. Gradually add powdered sugar and beat until smooth.
  3. Add cooled puree, salt, and extract (if using). Beat until light and creamy.

Step 4: Assemble

  1. Pair similar shells. Pipe buttercream onto one, sandwich with another.
  2. Chill overnight for best texture — they become chewy and perfect!

Tips for Success

  • Use aged egg whites for more stable meringue.
  • Measure ingredients by weight for best results.
  • Rest the shells — don’t skip it! They won’t rise properly without a dry skin.
  • Macarons taste better after resting overnight.
