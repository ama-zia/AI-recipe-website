Orange Olive Oil Cake

Orange Olive Oil Cake is one of those rare desserts that manages to strike a perfect balance between indulgence and elegance. The olive oil brings an earthy richness that’s beautifully lifted by the bright, citrusy notes of fresh orange juice and zest. This cake is soft, moist, and dense in just the right way – ideal for slow mornings, afternoon coffee breaks, or as a refined treat after dinner. It’s not just easy to make – it’s the kind of recipe you come back to because it always delivers. If you’re aiming to nail this bake, the trick is in the quality of your olive oil and using fresh oranges – don’t skip on the zest!

Servings: 8–10 slices

Baking Time: 40–50 minutes

Temperature: 175°C / 350°F

Cooling Time: Allow to cool completely before frosting (at least 1 hour)

Ingredients

For the Orange Olive Oil Cake:

  • 1 cup (240ml) extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 cup (200g) white sugar
  • ½ cup (100g) brown sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 tbsp orange zest (from 2 large oranges)
  • ¾ cup (180ml) fresh orange juice
  • ½ cup (120g) plain yogurt (or Greek yogurt / sour cream)
  • 2 cups (250g) all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt
  • Optional: ½ tsp cinnamon

For the Creamy Orange Frosting:

  • 225g (8 oz) cream cheese, softened
  • ½ cup (115g) unsalted butter, softened
  • 1½ cups (150g) powdered sugar, sifted
  • 2 tsp orange zest
  • 1–2 tbsp orange juice
  • Pinch of salt

Method

1. Make the Cake

  1. Preheat oven to 175°C / 350°F. Grease and line a 9-inch round or loaf pan.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together olive oil and sugars.
  3. Whisk in eggs one by one.
  4. Add orange zest, juice, and yogurt. Mix until smooth.
  5. In another bowl, whisk flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and cinnamon (if using).
  6. Fold dry mix into wet mix until just combined.
  7. Pour into the prepared pan. Bake for 40–50 minutes.
  8. Cake is ready when a toothpick comes out with moist crumbs.

2. Make the Frosting

  1. Beat cream cheese and butter until creamy and smooth.
  2. Add powdered sugar, zest, and orange juice. Beat until fluffy.
  3. Chill frosting for 15 minutes if you want to pipe it.

3. Assemble

  1. Cool cake completely.
  2. Frost with creamy orange frosting.
  3. Optional: drizzle with honey and sprinkle pistachios or extra zest.

Light, bright, and perfectly zesty
