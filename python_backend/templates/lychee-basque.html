Lychee Basque Cheesecake

The Lychee Basque Cheesecake is the kind of dessert that impresses without trying too hard. It's soft, floral, and creamy with that signature burnt top that gives it character. The subtle fruitiness of lychee cuts through the richness of the cream cheese beautifully, offering something elegant yet comforting. If you've never baked a Basque cheesecake before, this is a great one to start with—it's forgiving and doesn’t require a perfect crust or water bath. The key is in not overmixing and watching the bake closely near the end. The center should still jiggle when it comes out of the oven.

Serving Size: 6–8 servings

Baking Time: 35–40 minutes at 210°C / 410°F (no fan)

Cooling Time: Cool at room temperature, then chill for 4+ hours or overnight

Ingredients

  • 500g cream cheese, room temp
  • 150g (¾ cup) caster sugar
  • 3 large eggs, room temp
  • 200ml (¾ cup + 1 tbsp) heavy cream
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch, sifted
  • 100g lychee puree (see below)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

To Make Lychee Puree:

  • Use canned lychees in syrup
  • Drain ~8–10 lychees (no syrup)
  • Blend until smooth
  • Strain for a silky texture (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prep the Pan

  1. Preheat oven to 210°C / 410°F (no fan).
  2. Line a 6-inch or 7-inch springform pan with 2 layers of parchment, leaving overhang.

Step 2: Make the Batter

  1. Beat cream cheese until smooth and fluffy.
  2. Add sugar and beat for 1–2 mins.
  3. Mix in eggs one at a time, beating well after each.
  4. Pour in cream, lychee puree, vanilla, and salt. Mix gently.
  5. Sift in cornstarch and whisk until silky smooth. Don’t overmix.

Step 3: Bake It

  1. Pour batter into the prepared pan.
  2. Bake at 210°C / 410°F for 35–40 mins until:
    • The top is bronzed or scorched
    • The center jiggles softly
  3. Cool at room temp. It will deflate — totally normal.

Step 4: Chill

  1. Chill for 4+ hours or overnight for best texture.
  2. Optional: let sit at room temp before serving for a softer center.

Floral, creamy, and burnt in all the right ways.
