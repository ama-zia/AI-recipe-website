Chewy Matcha Cookies

There’s something quietly impressive about a cookie that blends the earthy bitterness of matcha with the soft, chewy texture of a bakery-style treat. These Chewy Matcha Cookies are exactly that — simple to make, but full of character. The key to nailing them is balance: enough matcha to shine without overpowering, just the right amount of sugar to mellow out the bite, and a buttery dough that holds its shape while still melting in your mouth. Chill your dough before baking — it makes all the difference for texture and flavor depth. And if you ever find yourself needing a healthier spin, a gluten-free version, or dairy-free swaps, our built-in AI chatbot can guide you through ingredient substitutions or generate a completely custom version that fits your dietary needs. Whether you’re baking for friends or just for yourself, this recipe is forgiving, adaptable, and seriously rewarding.

Servings: Makes ~16–18 cookies

Bake Time: 9–11 minutes

Oven Temperature: 175°C / 350°F

Cool Time: Let cookies cool on tray for 5 minutes before transferring

Ingredients

  • 2 cups (250g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp ceremonial or culinary-grade matcha
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¾ cup (170g) unsalted butter, room temp
  • ¾ cup (150g) brown sugar, packed
  • ¼ cup (50g) white sugar
  • 1 large egg, room temp
  • 2 tsp pure vanilla extract
  • Optional: 1 tsp vanilla bean paste

Step-by-Step Method

Step 1: Mix Dry Ingredients

  1. In a bowl, whisk together flour, matcha, baking soda, and salt.
  2. This prevents clumps and distributes matcha evenly.

Step 2: Cream Butter + Sugar

  1. In a large bowl, beat butter, brown sugar, and white sugar until light and fluffy (about 2–3 minutes).
  2. Add in the egg, vanilla extract, and paste (if using). Mix until smooth and creamy.

Step 3: Combine

  1. Add dry ingredients to the wet ingredients.
  2. Mix until just combined — don’t overmix.
  3. Chill the dough for 30–60 minutes for best texture and flavor.

Step 4: Bake

  1. Preheat oven to 175°C / 350°F.
  2. Scoop dough into 1.5 tbsp balls and place on a parchment-lined baking tray, spaced apart.
  3. Bake for 9–11 minutes, until edges are lightly golden and centers look slightly underbaked.
  4. Cool on tray for 5 minutes before transferring — they’ll set with a perfect chewy center.
